Architecture

Replication of Siddiqui et al. (2026) — two components that combine at retrieval time.

🧲 Embedding Component

Fine-tuned multi-qa-MiniLM-L6-cos-v1 on LLM-generated query–product pairs using MultipleNegativesRankingLoss. Products are indexed in a FAISS IVF-Flat index for sub-millisecond cosine similarity search.

🧩 Structure Component

Fine-tuned Flan-T5-small (seq2seq) converts natural-language queries into structured JSON filters: price range, review count, rating range, and subcategory. Applied before FAISS to preselect candidates.

📦 Data

100 Amazon Fashion products sampled from meta_Amazon_Fashion.jsonl.gz. ~10 synthetic queries per product generated by Gemini Flash (zero-shot), then enriched with price/rating constraints and structured filters.

🔁 Retrieval Flow

1. Extract structured filters from query (Flan-T5).
2. Pre-filter product indices by price / rating / reviews / subcategory.
3. Run FAISS inner-product search over filtered subset.
4. Return top-k results ranked by cosine similarity.

About

What is Eurydice?

A replication pilot of Siddiqui et al. (2026) "LLM-based Semantic Search for Conversational Queries in E-commerce", adapted to 100 Amazon clothing products instead of the paper's 1.3M cell phones.

The pipeline generates synthetic training data with Gemini Flash, fine-tunes two small models, and combines dense retrieval with structured filtering for hybrid search.

Key deviations from the paper

  • Domain: Clothing vs. Cell Phones & Accessories
  • Scale: 100 products vs. 1.3M products
  • Subcategory schema adapted for clothing taxonomy
  • Qualitative thresholds tuned via configs/data_config.yaml
